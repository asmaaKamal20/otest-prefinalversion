Introduction

UTEST is an integrated electronic testing platform designed to manage high capacity tests that require a high level of monitoring and security. It operates through authorized test centers across 70 countries. In addition, it provides home exams and distant exams while conforming to the international standards for monitoring and quality.

UTEST is designed using the cutting-edge technology and Artificial Intelligence to fulfill the needs of bodies such as ministries, embassies, NGOs and corporations; and educational institutions such as, schools, institutes, universities and training centers that mandate adherence to the international standards of assessment and testing.

Test Environment Standards

  Computers and system requirements:

  Display adaptor (minimum resolution 1024 * 768

  Free Hard disk size 50 GB

  RAM 2GB minimum.

  Sound proof headset and filtered Mic.

  Operating system (windows 7 - 8 - 10)

  Installing the following applications:

  Microsoft Silverlight

  Security patch

  Test Engine

  Test environment:

  Spacious test site free from obstacles

  Test taker must be alone in the testing room

  No test preparation materials are allowed in the testing room

  Test taker should sit down properly fitting the camera and should not leave the testing room during the test.

Test rescheduling and cancellation

  Rescheduling take place 48 hours before the test.

  Extra fees apply to rescheduling depending on the conditions of the test.

  Rescheduling and cancellation happen only through test taker’s personal account.

What to do on the day of the test?

  Taking a test at a test center:

 Test taker should attend 30 minutes before the time of the test to complete the registration process.

  Test taker must bring a valid ID/ passport.

  Test taker should read and understand test instructions form carefully and sign it.

  Test taker must bring the voucher sent earlier via email after completing the payment.

  Test taker must bring the receipt sent earlier by email at scheduling the test

 Test taker should exhibit a cooperative attitude towards proctors and test admins regarding inspections of personal belonging and keeping them in lockers.

  When test is taken at home.

  Computer and devices should meet the required standards.

  Test instructions must be read carefully and understood

  In case of technical issues, press help to get in contact with technical support

 In case of any cheating attempts, copying or photographing the test, whether directly or indirectly, the test gets cancelled and test fees will not be refunded.

Exam results and score reports

  Test takers automatically access the test results through their personal account on the platform. Other entities can also access the results through the platform.

  Test score reports use the candidate’s full name as spelled in the ID as registered; and in case of errors, contact technical support.

  Test forms are kept for quality and verification purposes; If your test form was by you or anyone else, the test results will be cancelled.

  Test results date: as soon as the test is finished, test takers can access their results through their personal accounts.

  Show test results: candidates can view their results and print it through their personal accounts.

  Test results verification: Anyone can verify test score reports using a verification code.

  Remarking: test forms are kept for remarking and verification purposes.

  Database security:

 Test score reports are withdrawn and cancelled in case of cheating or failure to abide by test instructions.

  Against photoshop fraud attempts.

  Repeating the test partially.

Test instructions

Test proctor is authorized to end the test, hide test results or block the candidates account if the test taker failed to abide by the following instructions:-

  1. Bring the original valid ID/ Passport.
  2. Cooperate with proctors during the inspection process.
  3. Sitting properly in front of the camera or wearing stuff that may hide his/ her face like a hat, a cap, headscarf or neqab (for women)
  4. Using banned stuff such as: test preparation material, mobile phones, calculator or other digital or electronic devices.
  5. Eating, drinking, smoking or side-talking during the test.
  6. Leaving the testing room before ending the test or during breaks.
  7. All attempts of copying questions or answers such as overhearing, recording, or communicating with others directly or indirectly.

Note

In case of technical problems or power cut, the test will be resumed where it stopped while safely keeping the remaining time, and test takers are not allowed to leave the room unless they have waited for at least 30 minutes. Then the proctor will fill out the status report and then the test taker shall decide whether to resume the test or repeat the test some other time for free.
